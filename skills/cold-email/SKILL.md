# Cold Email

Help the user write or improve a cold email. Ask for any missing details before drafting.

## Four required elements

Every cold email must have all four:

1. **Greeting** — use the recipient's correct title. Never use Miss/Mrs. for someone with a PhD; use Prof. or Dr.
2. **Introduction** — one sentence: name, position, affiliation, and what you want. ("My name is X. I am a Y at Z. I am writing to...")
3. **Context/Connection** — specific, personal, researched. Name the exact paper or project you're referencing and why it connects to your work. Generic praise ("I am fascinated by your research") signals a mass email.
4. **Call for action** — spell out exactly what you want the recipient to do: schedule a 20-min call, answer one question, review an attached CV. Make it achievable.

## Pitfalls to flag and fix

- Inconsistent font sizes or styles on paper titles → dead giveaway of copy-paste mass email; rewrite to be natural prose
- Sender name is an ID, nickname, or partial name → remind the user their full name must appear as the sender
- Vague ask ("I would love to connect") → push for a concrete, bounded request
- No formatting on a long email → suggest bold for key points, bullets for talking points
- Typos or grammatical errors → the email is a writing sample; proofread before sending

## Output

Draft the email, then add a short **Review** section noting any remaining risks:
- Title/greeting check
- Specificity of context
- Clarity of ask
- Formatting
- Estimated reading time (flag if > 90 seconds)

Keep the email itself concise. Most cold emails should fit in one screen.
